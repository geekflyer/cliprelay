Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 1, 2026

Overview

ClipRelay is designed with privacy as its core principle. We do not collect, store, transmit, or process any personal data or clipboard content. Your data stays between your devices — always.

Data Collection

We collect no data. ClipRelay does not have servers, accounts, analytics, or any form of data collection. Specifically:

  • No clipboard content is sent to any server
  • No personal information is collected or stored
  • No usage analytics or telemetry is collected
  • No cookies or tracking mechanisms are used
  • No accounts are required to use the app
  • No advertising identifiers are used

How ClipRelay Works

ClipRelay transfers clipboard data exclusively over Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE), creating a direct device-to-device connection between your Mac and Android device. No internet connection is required.

  • Pairing: Devices are paired via QR code scan, which exchanges encryption keys locally
  • Encryption: All clipboard transfers are encrypted with AES-256-GCM. Keys are generated during pairing and stored only on your devices
  • Transfer: Clipboard data travels directly between your devices over BLE. It never passes through any server or cloud service
  • Auto-clear: Clipboard content can be automatically cleared after syncing for additional security

Data Stored on Your Devices

ClipRelay stores the following data locally on your devices:

  • Encryption keys: Generated during pairing, stored in the system keychain (macOS) or encrypted shared preferences (Android)
  • Pairing token: A BLE identifier used to reconnect to your paired device
  • App preferences: Your settings (e.g., auto-clear preference)

No clipboard content is persisted. Clipboard data is held transiently in memory only during the transfer process.

Permissions

ClipRelay requests the following permissions to function:

  • Bluetooth (macOS & Android): Required for BLE communication between devices
  • Nearby Devices (Android): Required by Android to discover and connect to nearby BLE devices
  • Notifications (Android): Optional, used to show connection status
  • Camera (Android): Used only during pairing to scan the QR code. No photos are taken or stored

Third-Party Services

ClipRelay does not use any third-party services, SDKs, or libraries that collect user data. There are no analytics, crash reporting, or advertising frameworks embedded in the app.

Children's Privacy

ClipRelay does not collect any data from any user, including children. The app does not have age restrictions as it collects no personal information.

Open Source

ClipRelay is open source. You can inspect the complete source code to verify these privacy claims. The source code is available on GitHub.

Changes to This Policy

If we ever make changes to this privacy policy, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top. Since ClipRelay collects no data, meaningful changes to this policy are unlikely.

Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, you can reach us by opening an issue on our GitHub repository.
